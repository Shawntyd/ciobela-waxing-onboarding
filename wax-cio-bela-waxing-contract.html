Section I

Status of the Parties

This section establishes your classification as an independent contractor, not an employee of Cio Bela. Review each clause and check the box to confirm your understanding.

1.1 — Economic Independence
Contractor warrants that they are an independent business entity, in business for themselves, and not economically dependent upon the Salon. Contractor retains full authority over their own pricing, scheduling, and client relationships, within the bounds of this Agreement and Cio Bela Brand Standards.
1.2 — Tools of the Trade
Contractor shall provide 100% of their own specialized equipment and consumable products. This includes but is not limited to: wax warmers, magnifying lamps, wax, strips, cleansers, and any other supplies required to perform services. The Salon provides only the licensed space — not the tools of the trade.
⚠ Important: Bringing in or using another artist's tools or supplies without explicit written permission is a violation of this Agreement and may result in immediate termination.
1.3 — No Employee Benefits / Waiver of Claims
Contractor is not an employee of Cio Bela and is not entitled to any Salon-paid benefits, including but not limited to: health insurance, dental/vision coverage, 401(k) or retirement plans, paid time off, workers' compensation, or unemployment benefits. Contractor explicitly waives any claim to such benefits under Texas law for the duration of this Agreement.
Section II

Financials & Billing

This section covers automatic payment authorization and the salon's self-help remedies for non-payment or standard violations.

2.1 — Saturday Auto-Pay Authorization
Contractor authorizes the Salon to charge the card on file every Saturday for the following week's service fee. The service fee is earned upon receipt. No refunds or pro-rating shall be granted for vacation, personal time off, illness, or any other reason. Contractor is responsible for maintaining a valid payment method on file at all times.
⚠ Non-Payment: Failure to maintain a valid payment method, or a declined charge that goes unresolved by Sunday midnight, constitutes a default under this Agreement and may trigger immediate lockout per Section VI.
2.2 — Tier Sovereignty
Transition between rental tiers is at the sole and absolute discretion of Salon Management. Tier changes will be communicated no less than 7 days in advance unless the change results from a material breach of this Agreement.
2.3 — Self-Help Right (Maintenance & Restoration)
If Contractor fails to maintain their station in accordance with Cio Bela Brand Standards, the Salon may, without further notice, engage professional cleaners, repair technicians, or replacement vendors and automatically charge the Contractor's card on file for the full cost of restoration. This includes but is not limited to: deep cleaning, repair of salon property, replacement of damaged equipment or fixtures.
Your Responsibility: Keep your station clean, organized, and brand-compliant at the end of every shift. Think of it as your own business — because it is.
Section III

TDLR & Legal Compliance

Texas law requires strict compliance with TDLR regulations. The following obligations are non-negotiable and apply to all contractors operating within Cio Bela.

3.1 — Lawful Presence Warranty (Effective May 1, 2026)
Per updated TDLR rules effective May 1, 2026, Contractor warrants they meet all citizenship and residency requirements for Texas esthetician or cosmetology license eligibility. Contractor must maintain an active, valid Texas Esthetician or Cosmetology license at all times and must have it physically displayed at their station during all working hours. An expired or suspended license is grounds for immediate suspension from the premises.
⚠ Zero Tolerance: Operating in the salon with an expired, suspended, or revoked license is a violation of Texas law and this Agreement. It will result in immediate removal from the premises and potential termination of this contract without refund.
3.2 — Sanitation & Cross-Contamination (Double-Dipping Ban)
Double-dipping of wax applicators is strictly prohibited on Cio Bela premises. Re-using an applicator that has contacted a client's skin in the wax pot is a cross-contamination violation under TDLR sanitation standards and poses serious health risks to clients. Only EPA-registered, TDLR-approved disinfectants may be used on all surfaces and equipment between every client. Violation of this clause constitutes grounds for immediate lockout.
3.3 — CROWN Act & Professionalism Standards
Contractor shall comply with all Texas and federal anti-discrimination laws, including the CROWN Act. Cio Bela operates as an inclusive, welcoming environment. While the Salon does not control your service methodology, you agree to maintain the Warm Minimalism Brand Aesthetic Standard within and around the facility — including attire, music volume, and station presentation.
3.4 — Sanitation Mandate
Contractor is 100% solely liable for any station-specific TDLR fines or citations resulting from improper sanitation. You must use EPA-registered, TDLR-approved disinfectants on all implements between every client. Implements must be properly stored in sealed containers or UV sterilization cases. Contaminated implements must be discarded immediately per TDLR protocol.

Section V

Data Sovereignty & Trade Secrets

Cio Bela's client data and branding are proprietary assets protected under Texas law. This section outlines your obligations regarding confidentiality and brand representation.

5.1 — Exclusive Ownership of Client Data
All client data contained within the Salon's digital booking system — including but not limited to client names, phone numbers, email addresses, service history, and appointment records — is the exclusive Trade Secret property of Cio Bela. This data was generated through the Salon's marketing efforts, reputation, and resources. Contractor has no ownership interest in Salon client data, even for clients the Contractor personally services.
Note: This prohibition applies exclusively to clients who were sourced through Cio Bela's booking platform, marketing, or referral network. Contractor retains full rights to their pre-existing personal clientele. Any unauthorized solicitation of Salon-sourced clients shall result in $2,500 in Liquidated Damages per instance, payable immediately, in addition to any equitable relief the Salon may seek in a Montgomery County court.
5.2 — Client Solicitation Prohibition
During the term of this Agreement and for a period of 12 months following termination, Contractor shall not directly or indirectly solicit or encourage any Cio Bela client to transfer their patronage to any other salon, independent studio, or location where Contractor operates. This applies to solicitation via text, DM, social media, email, or in-person communication.
5.3 — Social Media Branding Policy
Contractor may use "Cio Bela" in their social media profiles and content while actively contracted. Upon termination or resignation, Contractor must remove all references to "Cio Bela" from their social media bios, highlights, and profile descriptions within 24 hours. Failure to comply constitutes Brand Infringement and is subject to legal action. Contractor may, however, retain portfolio content of work performed at the salon without explicit branding language.
5.4 — Booking Platform Policy
All client appointments at Cio Bela must be booked exclusively through the Salon's designated booking platform. Directing Salon clients to third-party booking platforms, personal booking links (Vagaro, Square, Fresha, etc.), or cash-only arrangements to circumvent the Salon's system is prohibited and constitutes a material breach of this Agreement.
Section VI

Liability & Occupational Risk

As an independent contractor, you assume full liability for your services, workspace, and occupational health. Review each clause carefully.

6.1 — Occupational Health & Toxic Tort Waiver
Contractor acknowledges the inherent occupational health risks associated with waxing services, including but not limited to: chemical exposure (wax resins, cleansers, pre/post-treatment solutions), repetitive motion injury, and skin sensitization. Contractor assumes all such occupational health risks and waives all future claims against Cio Bela for any respiratory, dermatological, or other health conditions related to the performance of their trade within this facility. Contractor is strongly encouraged to use appropriate PPE including gloves and protective eyewear.
6.2 — Client Service Liability
Contractor is solely and exclusively liable for all services performed on clients, including but not limited to: skin tears, burns, allergic reactions, adverse reactions to wax products, infections arising from sanitation failures, or any physical harm resulting from services. Contractor must ensure a signed Professional Service Waiver is on file for every client for every appointment. Contractor is strongly encouraged to maintain independent professional liability (malpractice) insurance. Cio Bela bears no responsibility for any service-related injury or claim arising from Contractor's work.
⚠ Insurance Recommendation: While not mandatory, Cio Bela strongly recommends that all contractors carry professional liability insurance (typically $100–$300/year through providers like Beauty Professionals Insurance or Hiscox). This protects you personally in the event of a client claim.
6.3 — Indemnification
Contractor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Cio Bela — multi service salon, spa & wellness center LLC, its owners, managers, agents, and employees — from and against any and all claims, damages, lawsuits, losses, costs, and attorneys' fees arising out of or related to: (a) any services performed by Contractor; (b) any injury, accident, or incident occurring within or near Contractor's station; or (c) any act or omission of Contractor or Contractor's clients.
Section VII

Termination & Lockout Rights

This section outlines the conditions under which the Salon may suspend, lockout, or terminate a contractor. Read each clause carefully — these provisions are immediately enforceable.

7.1 — Immediate Lockout Conditions
Per Texas Property Code § 93.002, Salon reserves the right to execute an immediate lockout and disable all digital access credentials — without advance notice — under the following conditions:
  • Failure to pay service fee by Saturday midnight
  • Double-dipping violations or use of other TDLR-banned practices
  • Conduct that causes direct harm to the Salon's reputation, staff, or clients
  • Operating with an expired, suspended, or revoked Texas license
  • Physical altercation or verbal abuse directed at staff, management, or clients
  • Material breach of any provision of this Agreement
⚠ No Cure Period for Immediate Violations: Certain violations — including non-payment, double-dipping, and conduct violations — do not require a cure period. Lockout may be executed the same day the violation is confirmed.
7.2 — Voluntary Termination / Resignation
Contractor agrees to provide 30 days written notice of resignation via email to the Salon's official email address. Early departure without proper notice may affect references or future standing with Cio Bela management.
7.3 — Guest & Visitor Policy
Contractor understands that Cio Bela is a professional service environment. While brief personal visits from friends or family are permitted, guests may not remain on premises for extended periods of time. Guests may come in for a service or stop by briefly to say hello, but may not linger at the Contractor's workspace, occupy client seating, or remain in the salon for hours at a time. This applies to children, family members, and personal friends alike. Cio Bela does not have the space to accommodate extended personal visits, and management reserves the right to ask guests to leave if their presence disrupts the professional environment.
7.3 — Legal & Dispute Resolution
Attorneys' Fees: In any dispute arising under this Agreement, the prevailing party — with priority rights reserved for Cio Bela — shall recover all reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs. All disputes shall be resolved in Montgomery County, Texas.

Digital Service: Service of legal papers, notices, and official communications via the Contractor's email address on file is deemed "Official Legal Service" under Texas rules and is as legally effective as personal service.
