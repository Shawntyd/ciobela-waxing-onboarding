Section One

Professional Appearance

Our philosophy: As a 1099 independent contractor, Cio Bela does not dictate your wardrobe. However, your appearance is a direct reflection of the brand experience our clients expect. We ask that every contractor uphold a standard of professional presentation consistent with Cio Bela's Warm Minimalism aesthetic.
The Standard
Contractors are expected to present themselves in a manner that is clean, polished, and client-ready at all times while on Cio Bela premises. Think refined, intentional, and professional. Cio Bela's aesthetic is Warm Minimalism — earthy tones, clean lines, understated elegance. Your personal style should complement — not contradict — this environment.
✓ Encouraged
Neutral tones, earth tones, or all-black attire
Clean, pressed, wrinkle-free clothing
Closed-toe shoes (sanitation & safety)
Hair styled and away from the face while working
Minimal, intentional accessories
Natural or polished nails that showcase your craft
✕ Not Appropriate
Visibly dirty, torn, or stained clothing
Graphic tees with offensive or controversial content
Open-toe shoes or flip-flops during service
Strong perfume or cologne
Clothing that creates a distraction from the service environment
Unkempt, unwashed, or unstyled appearance
Personal Hygiene
Given the close-proximity nature of nail services, personal hygiene is non-negotiable. Contractors must arrive clean, odor-neutral, and client-ready. This includes clean hands and properly maintained nails.
Section Two

Station Setup & Cleanliness

Your station is your business. How you keep your space tells every client — and every passing eye — exactly who you are as a professional. Cio Bela's environment is intentionally curated. Your station should feel like it belongs here.
Station Presentation Standard
Your station must be clean, organized, and visually cohesive at all times — before, during, and after each client. Products should be neatly arranged. Surfaces should be free of dust, product buildup, and clutter.
✓ Station Must-Haves
Clean, lint-free table surface at all times
Products stored neatly
Implements in sealed storage between clients
A clean client cushion/armrest for each appointment
Trash emptied before it overflows
No personal food or beverages on the service surface
✕ Never Acceptable
Dirty implements left on the table between clients
Product bottles with dirty, crusted caps on display
Excessive personal items taking up service space
Leaving a messy station at end of shift
Using another contractor's supplies without permission
Storing personal belongings in common areas
Sanitation Protocol
Per TDLR regulations and your Contractor Agreement, all implements must be properly disinfected between every client using EPA-registered, TDLR-approved disinfectants. Single-use items must be discarded after each client — never reused. Implements must be stored in a sealed container or UV sterilization case.
Section Three

Music & Ambiance

Sound shapes experience. The music and energy you bring to your station directly affects the client experience — for your client and everyone else in the salon. Cio Bela's environment is intentionally calm, elevated, and professional.
Music Standards
Contractors may play music at their station through personal earbuds or a low-volume personal speaker, provided the sound does not carry beyond their immediate workspace. Music must be appropriate for a professional, mixed-audience environment — no explicit lyrics, no aggressive or divisive content. Volume must remain at a level where normal conversation with a client does not require raised voices.
General Ambiance
Phone calls should be kept brief and quiet while with a client. Avoid loud personal conversations that carry across the salon floor. The environment you create at your station is part of the client experience you are selling.
Section Four

Client Communication

Every client interaction is a brand interaction. How you speak to, respond to, and manage your clients while on Cio Bela's floor reflects on every professional in this building. We hold ourselves — and each other — to a high standard of professionalism.
Communication Standards
All client communication must remain professional, respectful, and service-focused. Contractors may not discuss personal grievances, salon management, or other contractors with clients. Conflicts must be resolved privately and professionally — never on the salon floor, never on social media.
Conflict Resolution
If a client issue arises that you cannot resolve independently, notify Salon Management privately. Do not escalate conflicts publicly. Any contractor found creating a hostile, confrontational, or disruptive environment on the salon floor is subject to immediate review of their contractor status.
Client Confidentiality
Client personal information — including names, contact information, service history, and any personal details shared during appointments — is strictly confidential. This information may not be shared, discussed, or used outside the context of providing services at Cio Bela.
Section Five

Social Media & Photography

Your platform is powerful. Social media is one of the most effective tools in your business — and one of the easiest ways to create unintended professional consequences. This policy exists to protect both your brand and ours.
Photography & Client Consent
Always obtain explicit verbal or written consent before photographing a client or posting their image, hands, or identifying details to any platform. No exceptions. A client's service is private unless they agree otherwise.
Use of Cio Bela Branding
While contracted, you may identify yourself as a nail artist "at Cio Bela" in your social media bios and content. Upon termination or resignation, all references to Cio Bela must be removed from your profile, bio, and highlights within 24 hours.
Content Standards
Any content featuring the Cio Bela space, brand, or name must reflect our brand positively. Do not post content that is politically divisive, sexually explicit, discriminatory, or that portrays Cio Bela or its staff in a negative light.
✓ Great Content
Before & after nail sets with client consent
Salon aesthetic, your station, product flat-lays
Tagging Cio Bela on work done in our space
Behind-the-scenes of your craft
Booking links through the salon's official platform
✕ Not Permitted
Client photos without consent
Negative posts about Cio Bela or other contractors
Directing followers to book outside the salon's system
Using Cio Bela's branding after contract termination
Sharing any client's personal information publicly
Section Six

Guest & Visitor Policy

Cio Bela is a professional service environment. Every person on the floor contributes to the atmosphere. Our guest policy exists to protect that experience for everyone.
Personal Visitors
Contractors may receive brief personal visits from friends or family, provided the visit does not disrupt the salon environment or take up client seating. Personal visitors may not linger at your station while you are serving a client.
Children on Premises
Children are welcome as clients or when accompanied by a parent or guardian receiving a service. Contractors may not bring children to the salon as a childcare arrangement. Children left unattended is not permitted and poses a safety risk in a chemical-use environment.
Non-Contracted Individuals Performing Services
Only licensed, contracted individuals may perform services within Cio Bela. No contractor may allow an unlicensed individual to perform any service at their station without prior written approval from Salon Management. This is a TDLR compliance requirement.
